Geodesic Greenhouse

Solution: I designed and built a geodesic greenhouse dome with my team of researchers.
As an extension to my prior research on hydroponic plant growth, I needed a new location to house a larger scale research project. As the son of two architects, the only thing that would fly in our backyard would be something of architectural significance. So I built a dome.

Growing Colors

Previous Next
Question: Which color of LED lights do plants perfer?
Answer: White, like the Sun.
In an attempt to determine what the difference between white light and red/blue light combinations was, I designed and manufactured a few small light tents which would shine a certain color of light onto seeds I was growing. I figured the red/blue would be best because thats what many commercial growers and academic researchers use, but for me at least, that turned out not to be the case.

LED Aquaponics

Question: Can I create a self-sustaining micro-ecosystem with plants and fish?
Answer: No.
I took an old fish tank we had in out garage and I created a small aquaponic environment with LED lights, lettuce seedlings, and goldfish. I used a pump to filter the water past the plant's roots and then back into the tank. The pump got clogged and the taproot eventually clogged the drainage hole overnight, flooding our kitchen. Mom wasn't happy.

Printable Electric Cello

Problem: How do you practice an acoustical instrument without annoying your family?
Solution: Remove the acoustical element and print it on your 3D printer.
For my girlfriend's birthday, I printed her a remixed version of Connor O'Kane's electric cello. I wanted her to be able to practice her instrument in her house without her five siblings getting irritated about her loud instrument. I learned a lot about acoustics and the importance of accounting for tolerance in engineering schematic designs.

Slippery Slope

Problem: I didn't want to do a boring calculus project.
Solution: Teach yourself a new skill and do a project that uses that skill.
As a junior in high school, I was tasked with making a 3D visualization of an integral for my AP Calculus BC class. I got some of my friends together and we proceeded to calculate the volume of a ski slope as defined by 3D surfaces. I learned a lot about higher-dimensional mathematics and the analogies between lower and higher dimensional mathematical operations.

Build to the specifications of the Adafruit PiBoy, I worked with a raspbery pi and many other electronic componets to build a device that would play retro arcade games on a portable gameboy-like device.

Hackintosh PC

Previous Next
Problem: I wanted a mackintosh computer and we couldn't afford a Mac.
Solution: I built myself a PC that was specially designed to run Mac OS.
Probably one of the hardest things I have ever done, when I was 15 I build myself a PC on the smallest of birthday-money budgets with the intent on running a "hacked" version of Mac OS so that I could teach myself how to code and follow a tutorial that used a Mac. Looking back, a windows machine would have done the job. But the hard way is usually a better teacher.
